History of TechCon

TechCon began in 1996 in New Orleans as a technical forum focused on advancing electrical grid technologies. Organized by TJ|H2b Analytical Services, it quickly became a trusted event for utility hosts, engineers, and industry leaders focused on high-voltage maintenance and asset management.

In the late 1990s and early 2000s, TechCon expanded geographically and broadened its audience to include a diverse mix of professionals such as technology directors, insurers, and manufacturers. This growth helped build a strong technical community across North America through a traveling conference model.

Inspired by the original event, TechCon Africa was launched to accelerate the continent’s digital transformation. It emphasizes cross-industry collaboration, spotlighting blockchain, fintech, and AI innovations in government, business, and education. African editions now attract leaders from multiple nations and sectors.

In recent years, TechCon has become a premier global platform featuring keynote speeches, research presentations, networking, and pitch competitions. Modern conferences focus on emerging technologies like AI, HealthTech, SaaS, and Robotics, alongside policy discussions and youth-led innovation showcases.

Today, TechCon drives knowledge-sharing and collaboration at a global scale. Its impact includes fostering digital inclusion, influencing policy, and connecting governments, academia, and startups for economic growth. From its North American origins to its thriving African presence, TechCon continues to inspire innovation and shape the future of technology worldwide.

Our Mission

TechCon core goal is to foster innovation, collaboration, and knowledge sharing among technology leaders, entrepreneurs, investors, and researchers worldwide. It aims to empower attendees with cutting-edge insights and practical solutions in emerging fields like AI, fintech, and digital health, driving impactful progress and real-world transformation. The conference’s driving principles include inclusion, forward-thinking, and partnership-building, creating a dynamic forum where visionary minds unite to shape the future of technology collectively.
